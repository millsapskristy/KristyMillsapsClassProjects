Cat Facts

Before they were our purring companions, cats were fierce hunters in the wild. The African wildcat, their closest ancestor, roamed deserts and savannas, perfecting the art of stealth and surprise. This adept predator had no need for human company, thriving independently. Yet, its keen hunting skills and adaptability made it well-suited for environments where humans began storing surplus grain. As these wildcats ventured closer to human settlements, they found a new niche. Little did they know, their wild nature was the perfect foundation for what would become a domesticated life.

Communication and Behavior
  • Cats meow mostly to communicate with humans.
  • Tail position, ear movement, and posture communicate mood.
  • Scratching also serves as communication, leaving both visual marks and scent.
  • Pouncing, stalking, and chasing show natural instincts and social interaction.
